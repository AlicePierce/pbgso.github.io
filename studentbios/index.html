She is a biologist interested in understanding genotype-phenotype interactions in plants as well as immune and virulence factors of plant pathogens. She graduated from Sao Paulo State University (UNESP) in Brazil, where she also received her master’s degree in Genetics with a focus on genetic and QTL (Quantitative Trait Loci) mapping. ## Kelsey Wood

I study comparative and functional genomics of downy mildew pathgoens in the Michelmore lab at UC Davis. Our lab has recently sequenced the genomes of several downy mildews, including those infecting lettuce, spinach, tobacco, and chard and my research is focused on using bioinformatics to predict candidate effector genes and testing those candidates the lab to identify functional pathogenicity proteins. In addition to contributing to our understanding of the mechanisms of pathogenesis, the study of effectors will be useful for breeding disease resistance in lettuce. My experience in the biotech industry has taught me that scientific progress can be achieved by bridging the gap between basic and applied science. I believe that collaborations between industry and academia are vital to increase crop productivity and work towards improving plant stress resilience in the face of a changing global climate. Contact: klsywd (at) ucdavis (dot) edu

## Susan Moenga

Susan Moenga a Ph.D. student in the Plant Biology Graduate Group, is a member of Doug Cook’s Chickpea Innovation lab at UC Davis. Her focus is drought stress tolerance related traits and genes in wild chickpea populations that can be useful in breeding for climate resilience. Before UC Davis, she received her master’s degree in Plant Biotechnology from Wageningen University, in the Netherlands. While in Wageningen, Susan was affiliated with Ton Bisselling’s group, where she investigated the genetic underpinning of nitrogen fixation traits in Parasponia. She received her bachelor’s degree from Kenyatta University, Kenya and has previously worked with Monsanto, with the Water Efficient Maize for Africa (WEMA) project, developing drought tolerant maize varieties. Contact: smmoenga (at) ucdavis (dot) edu
